Saudi Cuisine

A rich tapestry of flavors influenced by history and geography

Traditional Flavors

Saudi cuisine is a rich tapestry of flavors influenced by the country's history and geography. From the aromatic Kabsa to the savory Mandi, each dish tells a story of tradition and hospitality.

Experience the warmth of Saudi hospitality through its food, where meals are often shared communally, reflecting the culture's emphasis on family and community.

Popular Saudi Dishes

Kabsa

The national dish of Saudi Arabia, featuring spiced rice with meat, vegetables, and aromatic spices.

Mandi

Traditional dish of meat and rice cooked in a underground pit, giving it a unique smoky flavor.

Jareesh

A savory dish made from crushed wheat cooked with meat, similar to porridge.

Saudi Dining Culture

Dining in Saudi Arabia is more than just eating - it's a social experience deeply rooted in tradition and hospitality. Meals are often shared from large communal platters, reflecting the culture's emphasis on family and community.

Traditional Saudi meals begin with dates and Arabic coffee, followed by the main course, and conclude with sweet desserts and more coffee. The famous Saudi hospitality means guests are always offered the best portions and treated with utmost respect.

  • Meals are typically eaten with the right hand
  • Communal dining from large shared platters
  • Generous hospitality and food sharing
  • Traditional seating on floor cushions
